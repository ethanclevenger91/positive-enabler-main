My philosophy as a Thought Coach is founded in The Tibetan Art of Positive thinking, known as Bön, a philosophy which pre-dates Buddhism by about 15,000 years.

Bön is the original source for what has entered into modern culture as the idea that "Conscious Affects Reality" sometimes refered to as "The Secret."

Ancient Bön teachings offer specific guidelines for how this works.

Applying these principles to your own life will help you see your life, your troubles as well as your triumphs, from a radically different, positive perspective.

I have been a student of Eastern philosophy, including Buddhism, Taosim, and Bön: the Tibetan Art of Positive Thinking for over a decade.

What does a 1-hour session consist of?

  1. The hour begins with a conversation about the specific items you'd like to work on.
  2. Through a series of guided questions, we will explore different positive perspectives that you likely haven't considered before.
  3. I will lead you in a guided meditation.
    This will have the effect of really helping you internalize and feel the new mindset you’re beginning to create in your life.
  4. After the meditation, the session closes with a few more guided questions
    These are intended to help you put into words and really solidify the new mindset you’re creating.

Thought Coaching is one part deep questioning, one part careful listening (to yourself) and one part intuition. All the answers come from you. I’m here to help you learn to ask the right questions and offer guidance along that path.

You possess all the resources, strengths and talents you need to create the life you want. Through our work together, you can learn to eliminate the should’s, gotta’s, have-to’s, must-not's, and yeah-but’s that are swirling around your head. You can learn how to transcend those discursive thought patterns.

Bönning: "Beeping Off the Nonsense"
Bönning is a technique I developed which can be an effective tool for learning how to ask some new questions and question some old answers that may be holding you back.

The participant wears headphones in which a simple series of beeps alternates from left ear to right ear, to distract the left and right hemispheres of the brain, which allows answers to arise from the "soul center" of the mind. The series of beeps is inspired by EMDR, a recognized form of cognitive behavior therapy. While wearing headphones, I guide you through a series of questions and positive visualizations.

You’re going to get some homework!
