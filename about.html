Tom Tortorich

is the author of 4 books, including his most recent, "Don't Read This Book! It might Cause a Mid-Life Crisis."
In 2014, Tom transitioned out of a 10+ year career serving the corporate world, and has since devoted himself to studying techniques for helping individuals restore balance in their personal lives and teaching the skills necessary for creating the life they know is possible.
Among other modalities, Tom has studied Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR), developed by Jon Kabat-Zinn at the University of Massachusetts, neuro-linguistic programming and EMDR.

Tom founded Positive Enabler in 2015. Its mission is to amplify the effect mindfulness is having on our culture. As Jon-Kabat Zinn cleverly ‘pun’-tificates, “mindfulness can help us come to our senses before it’s too late.”

Tom is also a freelance photographer (Scenes of Time.com), founded a publishing company (Green Effect Media) and previously ran a lucrative graphic design company, ttDesigns.

Micayla Harland

has been described as "leaving a trail of home & joy everywhere she goes."

Starting at a young age, she has inspired direct and positive communication between friends and family. Micayla has lived in 3 countries, and brings the best of each of their values into her practice.
Micayla is a student and advocate of logotherapy, a technique developed by Viktor Frankl, founded on the belief that joy is found through the search for purpose and the unique meaning of every individual's life.

Previously, she served the corporate world as a process improvement expert for more than 7 years, but found her efforts were falling on deaf ears of people who were "rather stuck in their ways and resistant to change." She now works to inspire positive change in those seeking a shift away from the status quo.

Where others see problems, she sees solutions and teaches the art of "solution-oriented thinking," and has a unique knack for finding a "win-win" outcome to resolve conflicts and relationship impasses.

All too often we get stuck looking at the problem, examining it from every possible angle but rarely applying the same diligence in looking for the solution. Micayla is well versed in flipping this paradigm on its head and letting the answer bubble to the surface.
